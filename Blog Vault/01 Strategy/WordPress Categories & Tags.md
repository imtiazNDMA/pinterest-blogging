# 🗂️ WordPress Categories & Tags

← [[Home]] · [[Site Structure]] · [[Content Pillars]]

Keep taxonomy **shallow and intentional**. Categories = your pillars (broad buckets, every post gets exactly one
primary). Tags = cross-cutting themes (use sparingly; don't create a tag per post).

## Categories (= the 6 pillars + seasonal)
| Category | Slug | Maps to pillar |
|---|---|---|
| Balcony Decor & Styling | `balcony-decor` | 1 |
| Balcony Furniture | `balcony-furniture` | 2 |

| Balcony Flooring | `balcony-flooring` | 5 |
| Balcony Lighting | `balcony-lighting` | 6 |
| Seasonal | `seasonal` | ✚ |

**Rule:** one primary category per post. Don't assign a post to 3 categories (dilutes the silo).

## Tags (cross-cutting — keep to a tight controlled list)
Audience/format themes that span categories. Suggested starter set:
`small balcony` · `apartment balcony` · `renter friendly` · `budget` · `boho` · `modern` · `cozy` ·
`makeover` · `diy` · `before and after`

**Rules:** reuse from this list; don't invent one-off tags; a tag should have 3+ posts before it earns its keep;
never duplicate a category as a tag.

## SEO note
- Set categories/tags to **noindex** if they create thin archive pages (until they have enough posts).
- The **pillar hub post** (not the category archive) is your main internal-link hub — see [[Site Structure]].
