## Reader & angle
Renter or small-space owner with a patio (or apartment patio) who wants a real, lush, productive garden but is
constrained by square footage, container-only growing, wind, heat, and patchy light. They want a *plan they can
execute this weekend* — not inspiration alone. Angle: "your small patio can hold a surprising amount of garden if
you plan by light, grow up not out, and pick the right containers." Practical, encouraging, second person.

## What "satisfies intent fully" (the moat for this post)
A reader should be able to go from empty patio → planted, thriving small garden without leaving the page:
1. Assess their space & light honestly (sun-hours, micro-climate, weight limits).
2. Choose the right containers (size, material, drainage, self-watering).
3. Know **what to grow** on a small patio — herbs / veg / flowers, split by sun vs shade.
4. Arrange it to look lush (thriller-filler-spiller, odd numbers, height layering, vertical).
5. Water and maintain it with low effort.
6. Save space with vertical & space-saving systems.
7. Keep it going across seasons / year-round.
8. Troubleshoot the small-patio problems (wind, reflected heat, limited light, pests).
9. FAQ mop-up of remaining queries.

## ⚠ YMYL plan — PLANT POST (read loudly, non-negotiable)
This post names plants. Treat every safety/edibility/care claim as YMYL.

- **Claims that REQUIRE an ASPCA citation:** any statement that a plant is **pet-safe / non-toxic / toxic /
  safe for cats or dogs**. Cite **ASPCA Toxic and Non-Toxic Plants:**
  `https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/animal-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants`
  and **web-verify the specific plant** against that database before labeling it pet-safe in the draft.
- **Default rule:** name plants **generically** and hedge claims to **light needs / hardiness / zone** rather
  than safety, UNLESS an ASPCA citation is attached. Do not assert pet-safety from memory.
- **Edibles — be cautious:** never write "this decorative plant is edible." Only call something edible if it is
  a recognized food crop grown as such; do not blur ornamental/edible lines. When in doubt, hedge or omit.
- **Care claims** (feeding, pest treatment, watering): hedge to "follow the plant tag / product guidance" — no
  absolute prescriptions.
- **No fabrication:** NO invented yields, harvest counts, days-to-harvest specifics presented as personal
  results, and NO "I grew this on my patio." Personal experience only in `[PERSONAL TIP]` placeholders the
  human fills. (Anti-AI-slop + content-quality guardrails.)
- Editor QA must flag any uncited safety/edibility claim as a blocker.
