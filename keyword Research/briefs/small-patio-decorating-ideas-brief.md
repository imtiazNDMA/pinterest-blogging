## Reader & angle
Someone who already has a small patio (rental balcony-patio, townhouse slab, apartment ground-floor, small back patio) and the basic furniture, and now wants it to *look* finished, cozy, and intentional — without renovating. They're scrolling Pinterest for copyable styling moves they can do this weekend, mostly on a budget. The angle: **"decorating," not "building."** Every idea is a thing you can add, swap, hang, or restyle — textiles, light, color, accents — not a layout or hardscape decision. Budget-aware throughout (thrift/DIY/dupe-friendly), because the cozy-small-space searcher skews renter and cost-conscious.

## YMYL / safety (verify, hedge, don't fabricate)
- **Plants:** any mention stays generic; do not give care/watering specifics. Light/zone claims hedged ("depending on your light and climate"). Defer all care to the patio garden post. Pet-safety claims → cite **ASPCA**; do not assert toxicity from memory.
- **Outdoor mirror:** caveat — avoid direct-sun placement (focused reflection/heat risk) and secure against wind on small/exposed patios.
- **Candles / open flame:** recommend flameless/LED as default; if real candles, note never leave unattended and **check lease/HOA rules** (many rentals prohibit open flame on balconies/patios).
- **No fabricated data, reviews, or "I tested this."** Personal claims only in `[PERSONAL TIP]`.
