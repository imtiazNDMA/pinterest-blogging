## ⚠ YMYL — READ THIS FIRST (writer: this is a plant post)

This post names plants. Every toxicity, edibility, pet-safety, and care claim is YMYL and must be **verified and hedged**. Non-negotiable:

- **Any "pet-safe / pet-friendly / non-toxic / toxic / safe for cats/dogs" claim REQUIRES an ASPCA citation.** Do not call a plant pet-safe from memory.
  - ASPCA toxic & non-toxic plant database (cite/link this): https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/animal-poison-control/toxic-and-non-toxic-plants
  - Verify EACH specific plant you label pet-safe against that database before publishing. If you can't verify it, don't make the safety claim.
- **Default hedge:** when naming plants, stick to **light needs (sun/part-shade/shade) and hardiness/zone** only. Phrase care as general guidance ("most herbs want 6+ hours of sun"), not prescriptive medical-grade instructions.
- **Defer detailed care/watering/feeding to the deeper guides** (this is a hub — route, don't teach the whole thing). Each themed section gets 2–4 sentences + a link to the specialist post.
- **No fabricated yields, harvest counts, or "I grew this."** No "my tomatoes produced X." Use `[PERSONAL TIP]` placeholders for any lived claim — the human fills them.
- **Edible / foraging claims must be cautious.** "Many herbs and leafy greens grow well in containers" is fine; specific "this is edible/safe to eat" claims need a credible source and a hedge. Never imply a decorative plant is edible.
- When unsure on any plant fact: **hedge and cite, or cut it.**
