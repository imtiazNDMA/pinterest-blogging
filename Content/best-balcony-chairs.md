# Best Balcony Chairs for Small Spaces

The best balcony chairs earn their footprint twice: comfortable to sit in, and small or foldable enough that you get the floor back when you stand up.

On a narrow balcony, the chair that folds to four inches and hangs on a hook beats the plush armchair. The plush one you shuffle around every time you water the plants.

Below are six chairs worth buying, each a different shape for a different job, with the trade-off named so you pick once and pick right.

## How to choose a balcony chair

Every pick below was judged against the five things that decide whether a chair works on a small balcony, not how it photographs in a catalogue.

**1. Footprint and how it folds.** Measure your usable floor before you shop, then leave a clear lane of at least 24 inches to walk through. A chair that folds flat or stacks protects that lane on the days you are not sitting out.

**2. Weight and wind stability.** A light frame can skid or tip in a gust on an exposed or high balcony. Favour a chair you can weight, anchor, or carry inside, and bring the lightest ones in before a storm.

**3. A weatherproof frame.** Rust-proof aluminum, powder-coated steel, UV-stable PE rattan, and teak hold up outdoors where thin coatings flake and untreated softwood greys and splits.

**4. Comfort for how long you actually sit.** A morning-coffee chair can be upright and firm. An evening-wind-down chair wants back support, seat depth, and ideally a cushion you can bring in dry.

**5. Storage and renter rules.** Folding and stacking chairs free the floor and slide into a closet over winter. If you rent, keep everything freestanding and check your lease or HOA before you fix anything to the wall, floor, or railing.

## What are the best chairs for a small balcony?

The best chairs for a small balcony are the ones that disappear when you need the space and still feel good for the hours you sit.

For most balconies that means a folding armchair as your main seat, plus a stackable chair or two for guests. Add one comfort piece (a recliner, rocker, or lounger) if your depth allows it.

Match the shape to your balcony's real constraint. Tight width points you to folding bistro chairs, a square corner suits a rocker or recliner, and a deeper balcony opens up a lounger.

## The best balcony chairs, pick by pick

### Best overall: a folding wicker armchair

**Who it is for:** anyone who wants one genuinely comfortable seat that still tucks away for cleaning, guests, or winter.

**Why it wins:** woven PE rattan over an aluminum frame gives you the cozy, textured look most people picture. Armrests and a seat around 20 to 22 inches wide let you sink in rather than perch.

Many fold to roughly three or four inches and hang on a wall hook, so the comfort costs you nothing in stored floor space.

**The trade-off:** a folding frame flexes a touch more than a fixed one, so check the hinge and a stated weight capacity before you buy. Lower-grade PVC wicker turns brittle in strong sun, so look for UV-stable PE resin.

## Folding and stacking: getting your floor back

On a small balcony, storage is half the value of a chair. A seat that folds or stacks lets the same square footage be a reading nook in the morning and a clear stretch for yoga or laundry in the afternoon.

Folding chairs collapse to a few inches and hang on a single wall hook or slide behind a door. Keep one hook by the door so putting a chair away takes a second, not a chore.

Stackable chairs are the better call when you want several seats at the ready but only sit in one most days. They pile in a corner under a cover and come down only when guests arrive.

## Materials and weatherproofing for balcony chairs

The frame material decides how heavy the chair is, how it handles weather, and whether a gust can move it. Here is how the common balcony chair materials compare on a small, often exposed space.

**All-weather wicker (PE rattan).** Woven resin over an aluminum frame gives the cozy look at a light, movable weight. Choose UV-stable PE resin, since cheaper PVC wicker fades and turns brittle in strong sun.

**Powder-coated aluminum and steel.** Light, rust-proof, and easy to fold or stack, which makes metal ideal for a balcony you reconfigure. The flip side of light is that it can blow over, so weight it or bring it in when wind picks up.

**Textilene and mesh sling.** The breathable fabric on zero-gravity recliners and folding loungers dries fast and does not soak up rain like a foam cushion. It is supportive rather than soft, so a thin pad adds comfort.

**Wood and teak.** Natural warmth that softens a hard balcony floor, with teak weathering to a silver-grey patina and shrugging off damp through its own oils. The cost is weight and a higher spend, and softwood needs sealing to survive outside.

No chair comes with a lifespan guarantee, since how long it lasts depends on your climate, sun exposure, and whether you store it off-season.

Buy the rust-proof frame, and store or cover it between uses. On an exposed or high balcony, bring the lightest chairs in before a storm rather than trusting their weight to hold.

## Best balcony chairs FAQ

### What is the most comfortable chair for a small balcony?

For pure comfort in a small footprint, a folding wicker armchair with a seat cushion is the easiest everyday win. It has arms and back support yet still folds away.

If you want to lie back, a zero-gravity recliner is more comfortable still, as long as your balcony has the depth to recline it fully.

### Are folding chairs good for a balcony?

Yes, folding chairs suit a balcony well because they give you the floor back the moment you stand up. Look for a rust-proof, powder-coated or PE-rattan frame and a stated weight capacity, since a folding hinge is the part that wears.

On an exposed or high balcony, fold light chairs away or bring them in during high wind rather than leaving them to shift.

### How do I stop my balcony chairs from blowing over?

Choose a heavier frame where you can, weight or anchor light chairs, and fold or stack the lightest ones away when wind is forecast on an exposed or high balcony.

Treat any manufacturer weight or wind guidance as the ceiling to respect, not a target, and check your lease or HOA before fixing anything to the railing or wall.

### What chairs are best for a tiny balcony with no space?

Folding bistro chairs and stackable stools win on the smallest balconies, since they fold to a couple of inches or stack in a corner and take almost no standing floor.

Pair two folding bistro chairs with a small round table, or keep a single folding armchair on a wall hook and bring it down only when you sit.

### Can I leave balcony chairs outside all year?

You can leave weatherproof frames like aluminum, teak, and UV-stable PE rattan out year-round, but they last longer covered or stored, and fabric cushions should come in dry. Real lifespan depends on your climate and sun, so there is no guarantee.

A simple routine of covering the chairs and stashing cushions in a dry spot does the most to keep any set going.
