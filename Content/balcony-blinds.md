## How do you choose balcony blinds?

Four things decide whether a balcony blind earns its place. Run through them before you look at a single product.

**Outdoor-rated material comes first.** A balcony bakes in UV and gets wet, so the fabric or slats have to be made for it.

Look for solution-dyed acrylic, HDPE solar mesh, PVC-coated polyester, or sealed natural bamboo. Indoor blinds in cotton, paper, or untreated wood fade, sag, and grow mildew outside.

### Light-filtering or blockout?

Decide how much light you actually want to lose. This is the call most people get wrong.

Light-filtering or solar mesh cuts glare and UV while keeping a soft view out and daylight in. It suits a balcony you sit and read on.

Blockout fabric kills the light for real privacy and cooler shade. Reach for it if low sun blinds you at the table, or the balcony sits next to a bedroom.

### No-drill mount or fixed?

Renting changes the answer. Most leases forbid drilling the wall or railing, and you want your deposit back.

No-drill clamp or tension brackets grip a railing top, a divider edge, or a door frame and come away clean on moving day. A fixed screw mount is sturdier in wind but only suits a place you own.

For more reversible upgrades, see our [renter-friendly balcony ideas](/renter-friendly-balcony-ideas).

### Width and drop: measure before you buy

Measure twice, because a balcony blind that is too narrow leaves a gap that sun and neighbors slip straight through.

Measure the full width you need to cover, then add a few inches each side for overlap. For the drop, measure from the mount point down to where you want the blind to stop, usually the railing top or the floor.

A blind that stops short of the rail still shows your ankles and chair legs from the street, so size the drop to the cover you actually want.

## Which balcony blinds are best?

Each pick below is a distinct type aimed at one situation. Match the one that fits how your balcony faces and how you use it, rather than buying every kind.

### Best overall for sun and privacy: an exterior solar roller blind

**Who it is for:** anyone on a sunny balcony who wants to cut glare and screen the view without going fully dark.

**Why it wins:** outdoor balcony roller blinds in UV-rated solar mesh block a big share of heat and sightline while keeping a faint view out and daylight in. You pull a chain or spring mechanism to set the height, so you screen the low sun and raise it back for an open evening.

These balcony sun blinds are the most flexible all-rounder here, which is why they top the list.

**The trade-off:** solar mesh filters rather than fully hides, so on a tight side angle a neighbor may still catch a soft silhouette at night with a light on behind you. Size up to the blockout pick if you need total cover after dark.

### Best natural look: a bamboo or reed roll-up shade

**Who it is for:** the reader who wants warmth and texture, not a flat synthetic panel, and likes a boho or natural balcony.

**Why it suits them:** bamboo balcony blinds and reed roll-up shades read as a far pricier upgrade and pair beautifully with plants and warm string lights. The natural slats filter light into a soft, dappled shade.

A roll-up cord lets you drop them for cover and tie them up for the view.

**The trade-off:** untreated reed weathers grey over a couple of seasons and can mildew if it never dries out. Buy a sealed or treated version, hang it where rain does not pool on it, and accept the patina as part of the look.

### Best for full weather cover: a zip-track PVC outdoor blind

**Who it is for:** anyone on an exposed or high balcony who wants to use it in wind and rain, not just on calm days.

**Why it fits:** a zip-track outdoor blind runs its edges inside side channels, so the panel seals tight and does not flap or blow inward. Clear or tinted PVC keeps the rain and wind out while holding the view, which turns a blustery balcony into a usable nook in shoulder season.

This is the most weatherproof option here by a distance.

**The trade-off:** zip-track systems are the most involved to fit, since the side channels need a solid frame to mount to, and they cost more than a simple roll-up. They suit a balcony you own or can fix channels to, more than a quick renter setup.

### Best for renters: a no-drill clamp-mount blind

**Who it is for:** anyone whose lease forbids drilling, or who wants a blind that lifts off clean when they move.

**Why it earns its spot:** no-drill balcony blinds use clamp or tension brackets that grip a railing top, a divider edge, or a door frame with no screws and no holes. You get adjustable cover that comes away in minutes, with your wall and your deposit intact.

It is the safe default for an apartment balcony.

**The trade-off:** clamp brackets need a lip or frame to grip, so they suit a divider or door surround better than an open, round railing. On an exposed balcony, a clamp mount also catches wind, so raise the blind in a real gust.

### Best for harsh glare or a sleep-adjacent balcony: a blockout roller shade

**Who it is for:** anyone blinded by low sun at the table, or whose balcony door opens off a bedroom that needs to go dark.

**Why it suits them:** a blockout outdoor roller shade in solid PVC-coated fabric stops the light for real, dropping the temperature in the shade and giving full balcony blinds for privacy after dark. Lower it fully and the corner reads as private and cool even with a light on behind you.

It is the one to pick when filtering is not enough.

**The trade-off:** blockout fabric also takes your view and your daylight, so it is best on the worst-glare angle rather than wrapped around the whole balcony. Raise it when you want the space open and bright again.

### Best budget quick win: a cordless fabric roll-up shade

**Who it is for:** anyone who wants fast, low-cost shade and screening without committing to a full system.

**Why it works:** a cordless fabric roll-up shade in outdoor polyester goes up in minutes and pulls down to cover a side or front sightline. As a roll-up balcony blind it is the most budget-friendly way to test where you actually need cover before spending on a roller or zip system.

It is the easy starting point.

**The trade-off:** lighter outdoor fabric is less durable than solar mesh or PVC and can flap on a windy balcony. Tension it well and bring it in over winter to make it last.

## How to mount and care for balcony blinds

Mounting comes down to what you are allowed to fix to. Renters lead with clamp or tension brackets, owners can screw a fixed bracket into the wall or frame for more hold in wind.

Whatever the mount, fix it to something solid. A clamp on a flimsy railing or a bracket in crumbling render will not hold a wet blind in a gust.

On an exposed or high balcony, raise or roll up any blind in strong wind. A lowered panel acts like a sail and can pull its mount loose or damage what is below.

Care is light but worth doing. Hose down mesh and PVC now and then to clear grime.

Let bamboo and fabric dry fully so they do not mildew, and bring lighter roll-ups indoors over a wet winter.

Check your lease, HOA, or strata rules before you fix anything to a railing or wall, since some buildings restrict what faces the street.

## Balcony blinds FAQ

### What are the best blinds for a balcony?

For most balconies, an exterior solar roller blind in UV-rated mesh is the best all-rounder, since it cuts glare and screens the view while keeping daylight and a soft outlook.

Pick a blockout roller if you need full privacy or dark, or bamboo for a natural look. Choose a zip-track PVC blind if you want to use the balcony in wind and rain.

### Can you put blinds on a balcony without drilling?

Yes. No-drill clamp or tension brackets grip a railing top, a divider edge, or a door frame and hold a blind with no screws and no holes.

They come away clean on moving day, which makes them the safe choice for renters. Just confirm the bracket has a solid lip or frame to clamp onto, and raise the blind in high wind.

### Are bamboo blinds OK outside on a balcony?

Bamboo and reed blinds work outdoors if they are sealed or treated for it and allowed to dry out between rains. Untreated natural reed weathers grey within a couple of seasons and can mildew if it stays damp.

Hang them where water does not pool, hose off grime occasionally, and reseal or accept the patina over time.

### Light-filtering or blackout blinds for a balcony?

Choose light-filtering or solar mesh if you mainly want to cut glare and UV while keeping a view and daylight, which suits a balcony you read or relax on.

Choose blockout if low sun blinds you at the table or the balcony sits next to a bedroom, and you want full privacy and cooler shade after dark.

### How do I measure a balcony for blinds?

Measure the full width you need to cover and add a few inches each side for overlap. Then measure the drop from your mount point down to where you want the blind to stop, usually the railing top or the floor.

Size the drop to the cover you want, since a blind that stops short still shows chair legs and ankles from the street.
