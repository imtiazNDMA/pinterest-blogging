## The two hard constraints (read first)

1. **Internal links resolve only when the target is live.** Every post links to its pillar hub. So **publish hubs before their spokes**, or early spokes will carry dead internal links for a while. (Not fatal — WordPress just 404s the link until the hub exists — but it wastes the SEO of those links and looks broken.)
2. **A pin is a dead link until its post is live.** Pins point to `bloomandlantern.com/<slug>`. The tracker's pin dates (starting 2026-07-31, now running to 2027) were **spacing placeholders**, not a real calendar. **Reset the whole pin schedule to begin ~1-2 weeks AFTER a post is published**, and never let a post's pins go out before that post is live. Practically: publish in waves, then schedule that wave's pins.

**Implication:** publish a solid *foundation wave* first (hubs + a few money posts), get the site looking established, THEN start pinning — rather than dribbling out one post at a time.

## Per-post pre-publish checklist (owner — the only blockers left)

Every Ready post needs these owner inputs before it goes live. None are QA blockers; they are yours to fill:

- [ ] **Pick the SEO title** from the 2-3 options in the HTML comment at the top of the draft; delete the comment.
- [ ] **Fill the 1 `[PERSONAL TIP]`** with a real first-person line (or delete it — do not ship the bracket). This is your E-E-A-T signal; even one genuine sentence per post matters.
- [ ] **Supply the 2-4 `[YOUR PHOTO]` images** — real photos or AI-generated (label AI on Pinterest, not required on-site). Set one as the **featured image**.
- [ ] **Replace `{{AMAZON_TAG}}`** in affiliate anchors with your real Associate tag (once approved). Until then, either hold the post or publish with the links pointing to plain Amazon search — but the FTC disclosure must stay.
- [ ] **Assign the WordPress category:** `Balcony Ideas` or `Patio Ideas`.
- [ ] Paste body, confirm permalink = the slug, publish.

> Tip: batch the boring parts. Generate all images for a wave at once; fill all `[PERSONAL TIP]`s for a wave in one sitting.

## Pinterest go-live (after Phase 1-2 posts are up)
1. Confirm the 12 pillar boards exist (you created them) and are keyword-described (`setup-checklist.md`).
2. **Reset the pin schedule:** ignore the placeholder dates in the tracker. Start scheduling pins **only for posts that are already live**, ~1-2 per day, newest-relevant first.
3. Order the first pins to match what you published: Phase-1 hub pins first (they have the broadest appeal), then the money-post pins.
4. **Seasonal:** hold `balcony-christmas-lights` pins (PB-047) for **Sept-Nov**; hold any summer-shade pins for spring.
5. Label AI-generated pin images as AI on Pinterest (home-decor GenAI opt-out).
