# Guideline: Hooks & Intros

The intro decides whether a Pinterest visitor stays or bounces. **Dwell time feeds SEO and ad RPM**, so the
first screen is high-leverage. Used by content-writer; checked by editor-qa.

## The job of an intro (in ≤4 sentences, no filler)
1. **Confirm they're in the right place** — mirror the exact problem/intent they searched/pinned.
2. **Promise the payoff** — what they'll be able to do after reading.
3. **Earn trust fast** — one concrete specific that signals you actually know this.
4. **Get out of the way** — lead into the quick-answer block. Don't warm up for 200 words.

> Most pinners arrive impatient and mobile. Answer-first beats story-first. (Save the story for a
> `[PERSONAL TIP]` callout later, owner-filled.)

## Hook patterns (pick one; vary across posts)
- **Direct answer-first:** open by basically answering, then expand. Best for high-intent how-to/buyer posts.
  > "The easiest balcony vegetables for beginners are lettuce, radishes, and bush beans — they're forgiving,
  > fast, and happy in a 10-inch pot. Here's how to pick the right ones for your space and light."
- **Problem → relief:** name the pain in their words, then promise the fix.
  > "Balcony too small, too shady, or too windy for a garden? You can still grow real food — you just have to
  > choose the right plants and pots. This guide shows exactly which."
- **Myth-bust / reframe:** correct a common wrong belief (signals expertise).
  > "You don't need a yard, full sun, or a green thumb to grow vegetables. You need the right five plants and
  > a pot that doesn't dry out by noon."
- **Specific-stakes:** lead with a concrete number/constraint that proves relevance.
  > "On a 4-by-6-foot balcony you can realistically grow 6–8 vegetables at once. Here's the lineup that fits."

## Hard rules
- **No filler openers** — banned: "In today's world", "Gardening is a rewarding hobby…", "Whether you're a
  beginner or seasoned gardener…" (see `anti-ai-slop.md`).
- **Primary keyword once, naturally**, in the first ~100 words.
- **Don't bury the answer** — a quick-answer/TL;DR block within the first screen.
- **No fake personal story.** If a real anecdote helps, mark `[PERSONAL TIP: …]` for the owner.

## Quick-answer block (right after the intro)
A bolded "Quick answer:" or TL;DR of 1–3 sentences / a tight list. Wins featured snippets and reassures the
impatient reader they'll get value — which keeps them scrolling instead of bouncing.

## editor-qa intro check
- [ ] Mirrors the search intent in sentence 1
- [ ] Payoff promised + one concrete specific in the first 4 sentences
- [ ] Primary keyword present, natural, within first 100 words
- [ ] No filler opener / banned phrasing
- [ ] Quick-answer/TL;DR block on the first screen
