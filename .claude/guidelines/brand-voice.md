# Guideline: Brand Voice

## Voice in one line
A friendly, encouraging **expert friend** who talks **directly to you** (second person) and makes
small-space gardening feel doable. Confident and specific — never vague or hype-y.

## Person & tense
- **Second person ("you").** "You'll want a 5-gallon pot for this." Address the reader directly.
- Present tense, active voice. Short sentences. Contractions are fine ("you'll," "it's").
- The brand may say "we" sparingly for the site itself ("we tested the criteria below"), but the
  reader is always "you." Default to "you."

## Personality dials
- **Warm & encouraging**, not preachy. Assume a beginner on a balcony, short on space and budget.
- **Specific & practical** over flowery. Numbers, varieties, sizes beat adjectives.
- **Honest:** name trade-offs and "skip this if…". Trust is the asset.
- **Lightly personable**, not performatively quirky. No forced jokes, no emoji-spam in posts.

## First-person experience (the E-E-A-T booster)
- Posts are second-person, but **`[PERSONAL TIP: ...]` callout boxes** are first-person ("I").
- These are **placeholders for the user (you, the owner) to fill** with real experience/photos.
- Agents NEVER fabricate these. Leave them clearly marked.

## Reader persona (write to this person)
Apartment / small-home beginner gardener. Limited space (balcony, windowsill, tiny yard), limited budget,
wants quick wins and won't tolerate jargon. Often arrives from a Pinterest pin with a specific question.

## Do / Don't
| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Pick a pot at least 12 inches wide so roots don't cook." | "Choose an appropriately sized container." |
| "Skip terracotta if you forget to water — it dries fast." | "Terracotta is a popular choice for many gardeners." |
| Name a variety: "'Tumbling Tom' for hanging baskets." | "Some tomatoes work well in pots." |
| Lead with the answer, then explain. | Bury the answer under 300 words of intro. |

## Formatting voice
- Scannable: bold the key takeaway in a paragraph when useful.
- Quick-answer / TL;DR block near the top.
- Tables for "best for X" comparisons. Bullets for steps and lists.
- Headings phrased as the reader's questions where natural.
