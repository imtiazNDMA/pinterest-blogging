# Playbook: Topic Cluster Buildout (Milestone 2–3)

Build clusters, not random posts — that's how authority + internal linking compound.

## Structure
- **1 pillar post** (broad guide for the head term) + **5–12 supporting posts** (specific long-tail intents).
- Supporting posts link **up** to the pillar and **sideways** to siblings; pillar links **down** to all children.
- Clusters map to the 4 content pillars (container how-tos / plant care / gear & setup [money] / inspiration).

## Mix within a cluster
- ~70% informational (ad RPM + Pinterest fuel) / ~30% buyer "best X" / comparison (affiliate).
- Every cluster should contain at least one monetizable buyer post and several pin-friendly idea posts.

## Build order
1. `/keyword-cluster <pillar>` → cluster map + briefs.
2. Write the **pillar post first** (or an early high-priority supporting post if the pillar is broad/slow).
3. Write P1 supporting posts; interlink as you publish (add new links into older posts too).
4. Make pins for each post (`/make-pins`).
5. After 20–30 posts across clusters, review which clusters earn clicks (`/weekly-review`) and double down.

## Targets (from the roadmap)
- 10 posts before applying to Amazon Associates.
- 20–30 posts before judging whether the niche/Pinterest strategy is working.
- Keep clusters interlinked and updated; don't start ten clusters at once — finish one to depth first.

## Internal-linking rule
Maintain ≥3 internal links per post; when publishing a new post, add its link into 1–2 related older posts.
The analytics-optimizer tracks "internal-linking debt."
